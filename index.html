apptopia // user graph page, app analytics

TL;DR

Apptopia provides app publishers & developers access to app intelligence worldwide. We at Create Studio worked closely with Apptopia, optimizing their product in a joint effort.

ROLE

UX design for user graph page.

I was asked to update this page to improve traffic. This means the value needed to be presented more clrearly.

CHALLENGE

The user graph page presents two major challenges: a wealth of information and it’s complexity.
This page was underused, and following conversations with users, we realised there were two major issues: users didn’t understand the information presented, and as a result, they felt this page gave them very little value. The way information was shown forced users to work hard for something they didn’t understand to begin with.

Users need to be able to both scan the page easily, and understand the intricate information given to them in as little time and effort as possible.

KNOWLEDGE DRIVES BETTER DECISIONS

To tackle this issue, I first needed to deeply understand what this data meant and what it stood for. As an outsider to the product, I needed to gain an understanding that was good enough to make good UX decisions, but I also needed to gain this knowledge very quickly, as I was given just a handful of hours to create a solution from start to visual design handoff.
My research consisted mainly of in-depth conversations with the company’s lead PM (users were not available to me), and trying to use the platform and specific page until I felt I understood it’s core value to users. Sometimes, time constraints mean having to rely on others in the product space for information, so I needed to be able to enlist the lead PM to this process, even though the PM is busy and overwhelmed with an enormous amount of tasks.

OVERLAPPING APPS - SUMMARY

The most relevant information to our users when trying to optimize app advertisement is to understand which other apps their users are likely to install.

At the very top of the page users can find the most prevalent app installed within the same category, and the top four apps in the overall top 2 categories. This information allows the app creators to understand quickly how to tailor their advertisement in a way that is most relevant to their specific users.

OVERLAPPING APPS - ALL

Next, app creators are given a list of all apps, with a pictogram in each listing representing how likely their users are to download this app.
This feature allows Apptopia to reflect a complex dataset in a simplistic way - just good enough to be of value, and simple enough to understand without drilling down further.

bits of gold // user activity page, crypto exchange

TL;DR

Bits of Gold is the longest operating Crypto-Fiat exchange in Israel. Last year, I led the redesign of the entire platform. In this case study I will focus on users’ active orders on their personal activity page.

ROLE

UX design, project lead

This area did not exist prior to the redesign. BoG asked to give their users a place to monitor their activity in a way that promotes trust and transparency.

CHALLENGE

This area in the legacy site was confusing and hard to navigate to. Users of digital currencies are normally suspicious to begin with, using extreme measures of security when it comes to their money.
Knowing this, I aimed to give our users a sense of control over their transactions with BoG.

This area needed to be both clear and concise, as well as rich and informative.

To maximize transparency and promote trust, I aimed to reflect to the user the most urgent information in a simple and intuitive way.
Users can easily understand the nature of the transaction (buying or selling), how much they paid in exchange rates and fees and when the money is due in their account.

RESEARCH

While designing the platform I spoke with both BoG users and potential users (those who knew of BoG but have decided against using the platform). During these conversations I tried to understand what drives users’ decisions whether or not to buy digital currency and specifically what drives the decision to use the BoG platform.
I spoke to 20 users, most of them male, under 45, from the Tel-Aviv area - a choice I made after diving into the current platform’s user data analytics. I also led a competitors research, to deeply understand and answer the question: what does a digital-fiat currency exchange look like in 2018? What should it look like? What should it encompass and what do users need?

ACTIVE ORDERS

Due to government regulations, Crypto-Fiat transactions can take anywhere from several days to several weeks to complete. This means users wait for some time after paying to see the coins reflected in their account.

In its minimized form (second order), an active order will present the user only with the most important information: what’s happening with their money, and what the next step is. The listing also reflects when the order was opened, to eliminate the user’s need to remember how long it’s been.

When expanded, the listing allows the user to delve deeper into the ‘nuts and bolts’ of their active orders. In this section I aimed to highlight the most interesting and important information, in an easy-to-read format.

ez-match // dashboard, corporate credit service

TL;DR

EZ-Match is a service dedicated to helping businesses and their accountants stay on top of their credit card transaction and fees. In this project we designed a dashboard reflecting information regarding irregular credit transactions, needing attention.

ROLE

UX design, project lead

This was a completely new product, aimed to alleviate workload for the EZ-Match account managers.

CHALLENGE

This project presented a challenge in two aspects: first, it’s end users are not proficient with technology, and are not likely to adopt change easily. Second, as a personal challenge, this area of accounting is esoteric and difficult to understand. Without understanding the material, designing a clear and concise interface would be impossible.

RESEARCH

To understand the needs and painpoints deeply, I spoke to all links in the chain of service at EZ-match - management, junior management, account managers and customers.
Research included two full days of talking to people and listening in on calls made to th EZ-match call center.
After talking with users, I tried to find inspiration in all kinds of report-creation systems and complex interfaces in different fields from HR and benefit management to financial management tools and more.

I was given many Excel tables that needed to be represented in this new interface, so my focus became trying to figure out what was the most relevant and critical information? What is relevant but non-critical? What needs to be left out?
In this space, I felt it was critical to understand what kind of interface would the “good enogh” product be, delivering high value to users at minimal cost to developers, as a starting point from which data could be collected to improve and expand the app.

IRREGULARITIES & LATEST REPORTS

The most frequently asked questions in the calls made to the EZ-Match account managers surrounded information regarding businesses’ irregular transactions: cancellations, corrupt credit terminals, fraud and more. To help accountants clear their workload without needing to call their account manager I decided to show them only their irregular transactions, in a minimalist way. No redundant, cluttered information and no complex, endless Excel tables.

Another area which created an immense amount of calls are reports generated and sent to businesses via email. Because of the medium (email), users tended to lose the reports in a sea of daily mail. This had an easy fix: allow the users to view and download their latest reports on-demand.

IN-PROGRESS CASES & UPCOMING REPORTS

Another much needed feature involved upcoming reports. During interviews on-premise, account managers reported they were constantly being bombarded with emails and phone calls asking when a specific report will be delivered. While reports need to be validated for accuracy and quality, report publishing is a cyclical event, and an approximate shipping date for each type of report is usually known in advance. I decided to display the approximate release dates in the accountants’ dashboard, for an easy fix to a painful problem.
